A student joins your survey but later wants to withdraw. What should you do?

✅ Correct! If someone withdraws consent, you should remove their data without making it hard — that's how you respect their autonomy.
❌ Not quite. Once someone withdraws consent, keeping their data goes against ethical research and privacy rights.
❌ Close, but not enough. Respecting autonomy means acting on the participant's request directly — not passing it up the chain.

Informed Consent Form Template

A short form to let participants know what you're
collecting, why, and what their rights are.

Why this matters:


Collecting personal data without consent can cause trust issues
and violate privacy laws.


Using a clear, short form helps your participants feel informed
and respected — and keeps your project ethically sound.

GDPR Overview


A full overview of what the GDPR means for your
project and how to stay compliant.

Why this matters:


The General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) is a European
privacy law that protects people's personal data.


If you're collecting or using any personal information (like names,
emails, or behavior tracking), the GDPR applies
— even for school or research projects.


Following GDPR helps you stay legally compliant, protect
participants' rights and build trust with users or testers.


View Overview

Tip: If you're not sure whether GDPR applies — assume it does. This guide helps you get the basics right.

Data Management Plan Template


A simple template to help you define what data you
collect, why you need it, and how you'll handle it.

Why this matters:


Collecting and managing data without a clear plan is
one of the most common ethical mistakes in ICT projects.

This template helps you think ahead — to only collect
what you truly need, keep it secure, and explain your choices
if asked.


A clear plan = better transparency, less risk, and smoother
ethics approval.


Download PDF Preview Online

Tip: Fill this in before you start collecting data — it's easier to fix mistakes early than clean them up later.
